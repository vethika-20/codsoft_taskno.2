The Lineage

A Century of Milestones

The moments that shaped modern luxury motoring.

1886

The First Automobile

Karl Benz patents the Benz Patent-Motorwagen, the foundation upon which Mercedes-Benz would later be built.

1904

Rolls-Royce Founded

Charles Rolls and Henry Royce form a partnership devoted to building "the best car in the world."

1913

Aston Martin Founded

Lionel Martin and Robert Bamford begin building cars in London, naming the marque after the Aston Clinton hillclimb.

1914

Maserati Founded

The Maserati brothers establish their workshop in Bologna, later adopting the trident emblem of Neptune.

1916

BMW Founded

Originally an aircraft engine manufacturer in Munich, BMW would later channel that precision into automobiles.

1919

Bentley Founded

W.O. Bentley sets out to build "a fast car, a good car, the best in its class" — and heads to Le Mans to prove it.

1926

Mercedes-Benz Formed

Daimler and Benz merge, uniting two of the automobile's founding companies under one three-pointed star.

1931

Porsche Founded

Ferdinand Porsche opens an engineering consultancy in Stuttgart that would later produce the 911.

1963

Lamborghini Founded

Ferruccio Lamborghini channels his ambition into building grand tourers with unapologetic Italian audacity.

Today

A New Era

Electrification and digital craftsmanship now write the next chapter — heritage reinterpreted for a new generation.
